About

Minecraft is a sandbox video game developed by Mojang Studios and originally created by Markus Persson, also known as "Notch." It was officially released in 2011, and since then, it has become one of the best-selling video games of all time.

In Minecraft, players explore a blocky, procedurally generated 3D world made up of different types of blocks—like dirt, stone, wood, and ores. The game features several modes, including:

  • Survival Mode – Players must gather resources, manage hunger, and fend off enemies (like zombies, skeletons, and creepers).
  • Creative Mode – Players have unlimited resources and can fly, making it perfect for building and designing without restrictions.
  • Adventure Mode – Designed for custom maps and challenges with specific rules.
  • Spectator Mode – Players can freely fly through the world without interacting with it.

Minecraft encourages creativity, exploration, and problem-solving. Players can build anything from simple houses to massive cities, working solo or with friends online. The game also supports mods, texture/resource packs, and community-made content, which expands its possibilities even more.

It's available on multiple platforms, including PC, consoles (like Xbox and PlayStation), mobile devices, and even VR.
